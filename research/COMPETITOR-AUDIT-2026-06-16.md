# Competitor Audit - 2026-06-16

This audit uses current live competitor websites to identify recurring category expectations for premium wig and hair-solution websites. It is not a copying brief. Wigs by Jolie should satisfy the category standards while keeping original art direction, copy, structure, and implementation.

## Websites Reviewed

- Milano Collection Wigs: https://milanowigs.com/
- Dorin Wigs: https://dorinwigs.com/
- Yaffa Wigs contact/consultation page: https://yaffawigs.com/contact-us/
- Daniel Alain / Follea: https://www.danielalain.com/
- Shlomi Mor Wigs: https://shlomimorwigs.com/

## Recurring Category Patterns

### Positioning

- Natural-looking hair is the dominant promise.
- Premium human hair, toppers, comfort, fit, and customization are repeated across the category.
- The strongest sites pair luxury language with practical reassurance: fit, color, security, density, care, and lifestyle.
- Consultation is repeatedly used to reduce uncertainty for first-time or unsure visitors.

### Conversion

- Primary CTAs are usually consultation-oriented: book, schedule, or contact.
- Phone contact is commonly visible near the top or footer.
- Stronger consultation pages explain who the consultation is for and what will be discussed.
- Several competitors mix ecommerce and consultation. Wigs by Jolie should not do that initially because the brief prioritizes private local consultation over catalog sales.

### Content

- Reassurance content matters: "not sure where to start," natural result, comfort, fit, lifestyle, color, and maintenance.
- Educational content supports SEO and confidence, especially around wigs vs. toppers, fine hair, thinning hair, fit, cap construction, styling, washing, storage, and care.
- Competitors often publish proof such as reviews, transformations, celebrity/media mentions, or institutional trust. Wigs by Jolie should not ship proof sections until approved assets and claims are sourceable.

### Visual Design

- Large hair imagery is the primary category asset.
- Premium sites use spacious product and model photography rather than dense text-only layouts.
- Many sites rely heavily on grid catalogs. Wigs by Jolie should avoid feeling like a warehouse by using editorial image rhythm and consultation storytelling.
- A useful recurring pattern is alternating broad visual sections with narrow guidance sections.

### Mobile Behavior

- Mobile pages need persistent access to booking or call actions.
- Long menus and ecommerce navs can overwhelm first-time visitors. Wigs by Jolie should use a short service-and-consultation nav.
- Form fields need large touch targets and clear labels.

## Exact Facts vs. Visual Estimates

Exact facts observed from page content:

- Milano prominently offers consultation and large ecommerce navigation with wigs, toppers, accessories, education, and showroom/pop-up paths.
- Dorin promotes 1-on-1 private virtual consultations and talks through hair goals, fit, security, color, cut, and custom tailoring.
- Yaffa uses a consultation/contact page with form fields and location/contact blocks.
- Daniel Alain positions hair-loss solutions, wigs, toppers, education, product shopping, and customer-review-driven proof.
- Shlomi Mor uses highly visible phone and booking CTAs, custom wigs, virtual consultations, product/category paths, and proof-heavy sections.

Visual estimates from crawler/page review:

- Most competitor home pages are image-heavy and conversion-heavy.
- Most use either a top sticky/utility nav or repeated CTAs.
- Product brands lean toward dense navigation; boutique/salon brands lean toward direct contact and consultation.

## What Wigs by Jolie Should Adopt

- Consultation as the primary conversion route.
- Clear "not sure yet" entry point.
- Service paths by need: wigs, toppers, extensions, styling/care.
- Fit/color/style/lifestyle language.
- Prominent phone and directions links.
- Educational section architecture for future SEO.
- Strong placeholder image hierarchy that anticipates real studio/product photography.

## What Wigs by Jolie Should Avoid

- Large ecommerce navigation.
- Fake proof, fake reviews, fake before/after images, or fake availability.
- Competitor images, text, code, layouts, or exact trade dress.
- Medical claims or treatment language.
- Financing, insurance, virtual consultation, product inventory, or price claims unless later verified.

## Design Implication

The original Jolie design should feel more private and editorial than the ecommerce-heavy competitors: fewer product-grid signals, more one-on-one guidance, stronger consultation rhythm, quieter luxury, and precise local contact paths.
