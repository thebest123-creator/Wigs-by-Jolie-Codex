# Wigs by Jolie — Website Project Brief

## Purpose of This Document

This document defines the business context, customer outcome, conversion strategy, design direction, content constraints, and project boundaries for the Wigs by Jolie website.

Codex must read `AGENTS.md` first and use this document as the high-level project brief.

Detailed implementation requirements are maintained in `BUILD-BRIEF.md` and task-specific documents inside `prompts/`.

---

## Project Objective

Build a premium, mobile-first, consultation-focused website for **Wigs by Jolie**, a boutique wig and hair-solutions business in Deerfield Beach, Florida.

The website’s primary business objective is to generate qualified private consultation requests.

The finished website should feel comparable in quality, confidence, usability, and art direction to leading premium wig-consultation brands while remaining original and authentic to Wigs by Jolie.

The website must not resemble:

* A generic AI-generated landing page
* A repetitive collection of equal cards
* A default Tailwind or component-library template
* A technology startup
* A medical clinic
* A discount wig warehouse
* A generic beauty-salon theme
* A large ecommerce catalog

## Primary Conversion Goal

The main website action is:

**Book a Private Consultation**

Secondary actions are:

* Call Jolie
* Get Directions
* Ask a Question

All major consultation CTAs should lead to one consistent consultation path.

The website should not divide attention among several unrelated primary actions.

---

## Customer Outcome

The website should communicate that Jolie helps clients find a hair solution that:

* Looks natural
* Feels comfortable
* Complements their face and personal style
* Fits their daily routine
* Provides appropriate coverage or volume
* Supports their desired appearance
* Feels personally selected rather than taken from a shelf
* Includes appropriate guidance on styling, wear, and care

Visitors should understand that they do not need to know whether they need a wig, topper, extensions, or another solution before requesting a consultation.

The consultation is positioned as the guided starting point.

---

## Core Questions the Website Must Answer

The website must help visitors answer:

1. Can Jolie help someone with my needs?
2. Can the result look natural?
3. Will the experience be private and comfortable?
4. Do I need to know which type of hair solution I need?
5. What happens during the consultation?
6. How are fit, appearance, comfort, and lifestyle considered?
7. What should I do next?
8. How can I contact or visit the boutique?

These answers should be communicated through page structure, copy, imagery, proof, FAQs, and the consultation experience—not through excessive explanation alone.

## Consultation Positioning

The consultation experience should feel:

* Private
* Personal
* Respectful
* Clear
* Reassuring
* Low-pressure
* Appropriate for first-time or uncertain visitors
* Easy to request on mobile

The website should repeatedly reassure visitors that:

* They do not need to understand wig terminology.
* They do not need to select a product before the appointment.
* They may choose “I’m not sure yet.”
* Jolie can help them understand relevant options.
* A consultation request is not a confirmed appointment until Wigs by Jolie responds.

Do not claim that consultations are free unless directly verified.

* Personal
* Trustworthy
* Established

## Visual Direction

Prioritize:

* Strong editorial composition
* Large, purposeful image areas
* Refined display typography
* Highly readable body typography
* Generous whitespace
* Strong section rhythm
* Varied section layouts
* Clear hierarchy
* Dark and light section contrast
* Authentic boutique presentation
* Excellent mobile behavior
* Purposeful rather than excessive motion

Avoid:

* Repeated equal-sized card grids
* Every section being centered
* Identical section heights
* Excessive beige-on-beige styling
* Excessive rounded rectangles
* Generic icons above every heading
* Small or timid typography
* Low-contrast gold or taupe body text
* Generic startup gradients
* Glassmorphism
* Floating decorative shapes
* Excessive animations
* Decorative interactions without conversion value
* Public placeholder notes
* Multiple competing primary CTAs

The page should use deliberate visual rhythm:

* Large followed by intimate
* Light followed by dark
* Image-led followed by text-led
* Emotional followed by practical
* Open space followed by focused detail

The same major layout should not appear in consecutive sections.

## Photography and Image Strategy

Photography will be added after the initial layout and placeholder system are established.

Until approved imagery is available:

* Use deliberately sized image placeholders.
* Preserve exact intended aspect ratios.
* Preserve desktop and mobile crop behavior.
* Assign each placeholder a unique image ID.
* Document the purpose, composition, subject, focal point, minimum dimensions, and crop guidance.
* Do not replace missing photography with generic icons.
* Do not allow missing images to collapse the intended composition.
* Keep image-generation instructions out of public pages.
* Use development-only labels for placeholder guidance.
* Prevent layout shift when final images are inserted.

Images may later be generated or supplied separately, but the website must be designed around a clear photographic hierarchy from the beginning.

The eventual image plan should distinguish among:

* Jolie
* Consultation process
* Wig styling
* Toppers and coverage
* Extensions and volume
* Boutique environment
* Craftsmanship details
* Client-facing emotional imagery
* Gallery and atmosphere imagery

Do not present generated or stock models as Jolie or as real Wigs by Jolie clients.

## Dynamic Feature Principles

Dynamic behavior should support usability or conversion.

Appropriate features may include:

* Transparent-to-solid sticky header
* Accessible mobile navigation
* Sticky mobile call and consultation actions
* Restrained scroll reveals
* Guided coverage-selection controls
* Service-interest preselection
* FAQ accordion
* Accessible gallery lightbox
* Two-step consultation form
* Form progress indicator
* Subtle image hover behavior
* Reduced-motion support

Avoid dynamic features used only for spectacle, including:

* Scroll hijacking
* Excessive parallax
* Cursor effects
* Decorative loading screens
* Constant marquees
* Unnecessary carousels
* Animated counters without verified statistics
* Exit-intent popups
* Autoplay video
* Chat widgets without an operational plan
* Animation on every element

---

## Consultation Form Principles

The consultation form should:

* Be short and approachable
* Work well on mobile
* Allow “I’m not sure yet”
* Avoid requiring technical wig knowledge
* Avoid unnecessary fields
* Avoid collecting medical diagnoses
* Avoid collecting unnecessary sensitive information
* Clearly explain that the appointment is not yet confirmed
* Provide accessible validation
* Preserve progress where practical
* Prevent duplicate submissions
* Include clear success and failure states
* Offer phone contact as an alternative

The form must not claim delivery or appointment confirmation unless a real backend confirms it.
