# Design Direction

## Concept

Private editorial boutique.

The site should feel like entering a calm, elevated studio where the visitor can be guided without needing to know the terminology. The visual language should balance polished fashion cues with practical reassurance.

## Art Direction

- Warm ivory backgrounds for calm reading.
- Deep espresso and ink sections for contrast and premium framing.
- Rosewood and muted gold accents used sparingly for warmth, not decoration.
- Large photographic placeholders with editorial crops and stable aspect ratios.
- Section rhythm that alternates image-led and text-led experiences.

## Interface Character

- Sharp, precise layout with modest 6px-8px radii.
- Buttons are direct, tactile, and high contrast.
- Navigation is restrained and service-oriented.
- Forms feel private, not transactional.
- Mobile actions remain easy to reach without covering content.

## Layout Rules

- Avoid consecutive repeated card grids.
- Avoid centering every section.
- Use asymmetry on desktop where it improves visual hierarchy.
- Keep consultation CTAs consistent.
- Make visual placeholders feel intentionally composed, not like missing assets.
- Use proof-adjacent language without proof claims until testimonials or reviews are approved.

## Public Copy Tone

Use concise, confident copy:

- "A private consultation for finding the right hair solution."
- "Fit, color, density, comfort, and care considered together."
- "You do not need to know whether you need a wig, topper, or extensions before reaching out."

Avoid:

- "Free"
- "Guaranteed"
- "Best"
- "Medical-grade"
- "Thousands"
- "Instant"
